# Strummer — Level 3→4 Training Montage
*Bard. Lugazil feast day, Tagmira.*

---

## Context

The party has been invited to Lugazil — grudgingly. Tagmira resents them and also feels safer when they're around. They're present in the crowd, trying to enjoy a feast day in a town that can't decide whether to thank them or blame them.

The flyting is the evening entertainment. Strummer doesn't know what's coming.

---

## The Ambush

**Ossivan Carn** — a traveling lay-judge of Lugus, originally from Velgroth, working a circuit through the Marches. He's available at feast days for disputes and arbitration. He reads the room immediately. He sees the party. He sees Strummer.

He selects him as the flyting opponent. It isn't personal. Strummer is simply the best available material — a bard, a performer, visibly part of a group the crowd already resents. Ossivan frames the flyting as a formal reckoning. The weight of Lugazil sits behind it. The crowd isn't just watching entertainment. They're watching something that feels like judgment.

The other party members are in the crowd. Visible.

---

## The Charge — Options

Ossivan's opening. Choose one, or let the moment decide:

> *"The Brambles walked before you came. They walk further since."*

> *"By what right did strangers disturb what Tagmira had learned to leave alone?"*

> *"We thank you for the Bronze Host's retreat. We ask who invited them to our hunting grounds in the first place."*

> *"A bard travels with fighters and calls himself apart from their deeds. Name one thing your songs have protected."*

> *"These are the heroes of Tagmira. Our dead are in the Brambles. Connect those two facts however you like."*

> *"Tell me what Tagmira looked like before you arrived. Then tell me what it looks like now."*

---

## The Shape of the Scene

Three rounds. The crowd starts hostile.

**Round 1** — Ossivan establishes the charge with Lugus's authority behind it. Formal, precise, crowd-pleasing. Strummer takes the hit.

**Round 2** — Strummer finds his footing. How is up to Hoi.

**Round 3** — Something turns. Either the crowd shifts, or it doesn't, but something is decided.

*Do not presuppose Strummer's approach. Leave rounds 2 and 3 entirely open.*

---

## The Level

The crowd goes quiet. Then something shifts — not love, not forgiveness. Recognition.

Strummer is now a known quantity in Tagmira. In a frightened town that half-blames his party, that's a weapon and a problem at once.

---

## Ossivan Carn — Quick Reference

*Lay-judge. Traveling. Originally Velgroth. Long-term recurring antagonist.*

Well-dressed for the road. Clean hands. Writes everything down in a small bound ledger inside his coat. Genuinely believes he is helping. Is not wrong often enough to be dismissed.

Wants the record to be accurate, his reputation to precede him, repeat business.

Does not want people who won't accept a fair verdict.

Excellent memory for names, debts, and slights — his own and others'.

Once arbitrated a land dispute between two families feuding for thirty years. Both left furious. Both complied. He considers this a success. Was run out of a village near Lucernia after a verdict went badly wrong — he maintains the verdict was correct and the village was wrong to ignore it.

*The most impartial man in any room.*

**Using him going forward:** he can be hired, cited, or pointed at enemies. Lugus's law is neutral and Ossivan follows it. The problem is he cannot be controlled once engaged, and he keeps notes on everyone.
