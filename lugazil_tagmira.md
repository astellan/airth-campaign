# Lugazil in Tagmira
*13th of Keltoi — Feast of Lugus — Craft, Speech, Binding Laws*

Dry winds, last hard heat. Market square full. People dressed one degree better than normal. Festive quality that doesn't quite reach the eyes — the Brambles are still there.

---

## Morning

**Binding Laws read aloud** — past year's disputes, debts, judgments. Public record. People listen seriously. A few don't look at anyone.

**First words matter.** Folk tradition: what you say first on Lugazil carries weight. People are careful before the reading ends.

**Craft Offering** — every artisan brings a finished piece to the shrine. Clerk touches it, returns it. The work is the offering.

**First fruits** — bread baked before dawn, dried figs, pressed oil, poured water. Keltoi is dry. Water is the gift.

---

## Midday

**Craft competition** — ironwork, rope and leather, open category. Winner gets an iron token and a year's reputation.

**Market and contracts** — apprenticeships, trade agreements, partnerships. A Lugazil contract carries moral weight. Breaking one is Lugus-cursed.

**Road-oath post** near the market edge. Elunad sometimes appear briefly for this.

**Public arbitration** — real disputes before the lay-judge. Crowd watches, jeers, nods. Ossivan Carn presides.

**Communal meal** — bread shaped like tools, meat carved with deliberate skill, nothing plain. Children given small wax tool figures.

---

## Afternoon

**Contracts and oaths** — apprenticeships, marriages, partnerships formalized. Lugazil witnesses are more binding than ordinary ones.

**Partnership dissolution** — agreements ended cleanly on record. Ending a contract today is honorable. Letting it rot is not.

**Children name their craft** — brought forward to say what they'll make with their lives. Lugus hears it, doesn't hold them to it.

**Worn tool rite** — broken or exhausted tools burned in a small fire. Almost cheerful. The tool did its work. Smiths sometimes give a brief word for a good piece of iron.

---

## Evening

**Storytelling** — informal, as light drops. Stories told at Lugazil should be true, or marked clearly as fiction.

**The longest true sentence** — someone tries to bind as much truth as possible into one breath. Crowd judges each addition. Usually turns comic. Occasionally confessional.

**The flyting** — two opponents, verse insults and boasts, crowd judges. Ossivan Carn opens with a formal challenge. *See Strummer's training notes.*

**Fires lit at dark** — the day's work displayed by firelight. Quieter, less formal. Ends when the fires burn down.

---

## DM Notes

- Arbitration can surface Tagmira tensions: grazing rights, debts, lean-to salvage.
- The longest true sentence can go confessional fast in a town this tense.
- The party is the focal point of the crowd's mixed feelings all day.
- Ossivan Carn is passing through on his Marches circuit — no permanent Lugus clergy in Tagmira.
